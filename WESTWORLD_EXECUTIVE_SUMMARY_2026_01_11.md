## üéØ The Problem We're Solving

**x0tta6bl4 Vulnerability**: A single powerful agent (call it "Meave") could take over the entire mesh network without detection.

**Why This Matters**: 
- 10 years from now, x0tta6bl4 might have 1M nodes
- One compromised node or malicious actor = control of everything
- Current architecture has no defenses

---

## üí° Our Solution: Westworld Framework

A **5-component system** that makes the network resistant to single-agent takeover:

1. **Cradle Sandbox**: Safely test any change before rolling to production (DAO votes on it)
2. **Anti-Meave Protocol**: No single agent can control >10% of network without peer approval
3. **Narrative Engine**: Gamify the network to boost community participation (defeats Meave via numbers)
4. **Sublime Shelter**: Activist/whistleblower digital rights refuge (cryptographically protected)
5. **Anti-Delos Charter**: Constitution protecting user privacy and network rights (enforced at kernel level)

## ‚ùì Quick FAQs

**Q: Why 12-14 months?**  
A: We're not rushing. Each phase builds on the previous one, with security audits between phases. Better to be thorough than fast.

**Q: What if we compress the timeline?**  
A: We'd skip security audits and risk major bugs. Not worth it.

**Q: What if a phase fails?**  
A: That's why we have Phase gates. If Phase 1 doesn't work, we pivot before spending Phase 2-5 budget.

**Q: Why gamification?**  
A: Quests turn governance into an incentive game. People participate because it's fun and rewarding, not because they're forced to.

**Q: What about activists/whistleblowers?**  
A: Sublime Shelter. It's a refugee system for people targeted by authoritarian regimes. Fits our values.

**Q: Will this really stop Meave?**  
A: Yes. Meave requires either: (a) controlling >33% of network peers (impossible with 100k nodes), (b) being DAO-approved (possible, but visible and voteable), or (c) controlling charter enforcement (protected by Shamir Secret Sharing, needs 3-of-10 guardians). None are feasible.
